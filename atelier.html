How We Work

From hide to
finished object

01

Leather Selection

2–3 weeks

Every hide is inspected in person at Conceria Walpier in Santa Croce. Céleste makes the journey herself, twice a year. A hide is selected for each piece based on its grain pattern, its thickness at the spine, and what she calls "its willingness to be something."

02

Pattern & Cutting

3–5 days

Marco Ferretti draws the pattern directly onto the leather in silver pen, reading the hide's natural topography. Panels are cut with a round knife — no machine, no die. The edges are immediately bevelled and burnished before they can absorb ambient moisture.

03

Preparation

1–2 days

Cut edges are treated with a first application of gum tragacanth and burnished warm with a wooden bone folder. Panels destined for lining are skived to a uniform 0.6mm on the flesh side. All surfaces are conditioned with a neatsfoot oil compound.

04

Saddle Stitching

4–8 days

Every stitch is made by hand using the Goubard two-needle method. Linen thread is waxed with a beeswax block before each session. Isabeau stitches at 8 stitches per centimetre — twice the industry standard. Each stitch is locked individually. There is no bobbin to unravel.

05

Hardware Setting

1–2 days

All hardware is sourced from a family foundry in Arezzo and plated to specification. Tomás sets each piece using a custom jig to achieve exact alignment. He checks the action of every clasp and strap attachment against a torque specification he developed himself over nine years.

06

Edge Finishing

2–4 days

Yuki applies edge paint in layers — between twelve and eighteen coats depending on the leather weight — sanding between each application with progressively finer abrasive paper. The final coat is burnished with a heated wooden tool until the edge becomes a continuous, imperceptible transition from the face.

07

Conditioning & Review

3–5 days

The finished piece rests for three days before Céleste reviews it. She examines every centimetre under a magnifying loupe. If a stitch is proud by more than 0.1mm, the section is re-done. There is no such thing as close enough.

08

Signing & Certification

1 day

The artisan who completed the piece stamps their initials on the interior, beneath the lining pocket. A certificate of completion is hand-written, including the hide origin, the date of completion, and the name of the client it was made for. Then it is wrapped in unbleached cotton and sealed with wax.

What We Use

Materials chosen
for permanence

Primary Material

Vegetable-Tanned
Full-Grain Calfskin

Sourced exclusively from Conceria Walpier in Santa Croce sull'Arno, Tuscany. Tanned over 60 days in pits of oak bark extract. Full-grain means the surface has never been sanded, corrected, or coated. Every mark the animal carried is present. Every mark you add becomes part of its character.

Thread

Beeswax-Treated
Linen Thread

Four-strand linen thread from a mill in Vendôme, France. Waxed by hand before each stitching session — the heat of friction further embedding the wax. Where a machine stitch creates a single interlocking loop that fails along its entire length when broken, a hand saddle stitch locks each stitch individually. One broken stitch remains one broken stitch.
