1961
Our History
1961

Founded by Émile Aurèle

A former apprentice at a Florentine saddlery, Émile Aurèle opens a single-room workshop on Via dei Serragli with one rule: finish one piece before beginning the next. That year, he completes four bags.

1974

The Quarterly Charter

After a commission from a Milanese countess requires eighteen months of refinement, Émile formally adopts the quarterly model. Three pieces. No more. The charter is signed and framed in the atelier — where it remains.

1989

The Leather Accord

Aurèle enters an exclusive partnership with Conceria Walpier in Santa Croce sull'Arno — one of the last tanneries still practising traditional vegetable tanning over sixty days. A relationship that continues unchanged.

2003

Passed to the Second Generation

Émile's daughter, Céleste Aurèle, assumes creative direction. She studies under the last saddle-stitching masters in Périgueux before returning to Florence with one addition to her father's charter: every piece must have a story worth telling.

2026

Sixty-Five Years. Unchanged.

The atelier still occupies the original building on Via dei Serragli. The charter still hangs on the wall. Three pieces, every quarter. Nothing more has ever needed to change.

Our Values
What We Believe

The principles that
have never wavered

I
Restraint as Mastery

We believe that the discipline to stop is harder than the desire to continue. Three pieces is not a marketing decision. It is an ethical one.

II
Material Honesty

We use full-grain leather because it is the truest form. It shows age. It develops character. It cannot be faked. We buy only what we can trace to its source.

III
The Long Relationship

We restore every piece we have ever made, for the life of its first owner. This is not generosity. It is the only way to make something with genuine permanence.

IV
Named Work

Every piece carries the name of its maker inside. Anonymity is for factories. Our craftspeople sign their work as artists sign a canvas.

V
No Advertising

Aurèle has never placed an advertisement. Our clients find us through other clients. We prefer it this way. It ensures the right conversation reaches the right people.

VI
Silence Over Noise

We do not comment on trends. We do not react to seasons. We make what we believe is worth making, when we believe it is ready, and we offer it without urgency.

Creative Direction

Céleste Aurèle

Creative Director & Master Artisan · Since 2003

"My father taught me that the most dangerous moment in making anything is when you think it is finished. That is when you must look again, harder, longer, with more doubt."

Céleste trained under master cordwainer Jean-Pierre Fabre in Périgueux from 1994 to 2001, learning the Goubard saddle-stitch — a technique requiring two needles, one hand, and years of practice before the tension becomes instinctive.

The People

Those who make

Marco Ferretti

Lead Cutter · 22 years

Marco trained at the Bottega Orafi school in Florence. He cuts every panel freehand, using a template only as a reference. "The leather tells you where to cut," he says. "The template is just in case you stop listening."

Isabeau Renard

Saddle Stitch · 14 years

A former surgical suture technician from Lyon, Isabeau came to Aurèle after a decade in operating theatres. Her tension is flawless. She stitches at 8 stitches per centimetre — twice the standard — and finishes every thread end by hand.

Tomás Vega

Hardware & Finishing · 9 years

Tomás trained as a silversmith in Toledo before specialising in hardware setting. He hand-polishes every clasp through seven grades of abrasive before it leaves his bench. He considers anything less "a beginning, not a finish."

Yuki Tanaka

Edge Painting · 11 years

Edge painting is the invisible work — the burnished, layered finish applied to every raw leather edge. Yuki applies between twelve and eighteen coats depending on the leather weight, building a surface that feels continuous with the hide itself.
